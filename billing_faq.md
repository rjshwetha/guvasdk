# Sarva Health Patient Billing FAQ

This document is used by the Billing Statement agent to answer common patient billing questions during outbound statement notification calls.

---

## Why do I owe money after my insurance paid?

Insurance plans typically require patients to pay a portion of medical costs. Your balance represents your **patient responsibility** — the share your insurance plan determined you owe based on your benefits.

Common reasons you may owe:
- **Deductible:** Your plan requires you to pay a set annual amount before insurance covers most services. If you haven't met your deductible for the year, you pay first.
- **Coinsurance:** After your deductible, you may owe a percentage of costs (e.g., 20%) while insurance pays the rest (e.g., 80%).
- **Copay:** A flat fee per visit set by your plan (e.g., $30 for a specialist visit).
- **Non-covered services:** Some services or procedures are excluded from your plan's coverage.
- **Out-of-network care:** If your provider is out of your plan's network, you may owe significantly more.

---

## What is a deductible?

A **deductible** is the amount you pay for covered healthcare services before your insurance plan starts to pay. For example, if your deductible is $1,500, you pay the first $1,500 of covered services each plan year. After that, you usually pay only a copay or coinsurance.

Deductibles reset at the start of each plan year (often January 1st).

## How do I read my Explanation of Benefits (EOB)?

An EOB is a statement from your insurance company explaining how a claim was processed. It is **not a bill**. Key sections to look for:

- **Amount billed:** What the provider charged
- **Amount allowed:** What your plan agrees to pay (contracted rate)
- **Plan paid:** What insurance covered
- **Your responsibility:** What you owe the provider

If the amount you owe on our statement matches the "Your responsibility" column on your EOB, the billing is correct.

---

## Why is my bill different from what the EOB shows?

This can happen if:
- You have secondary insurance that wasn't billed yet
- A payment you made hasn't been applied yet
- The EOB reflects a different claim or date of service

If you believe there's a discrepancy, our billing team can review your account and compare it to the EOB.

## Can I set up a payment plan?

Yes. Sarva Health offers payment plan options for patients who need to pay their balance over time. Please ask to be connected with a billing specialist who can set up a plan that works for your situation. No additional fees are charged for standard payment plans.

---

## How do I pay online?

You can pay your balance securely through the patient portal. Log in at **portal.sarvahealth.com** using your patient account credentials. If you haven't registered, you can create an account using the information from your statement.

---

## What if I believe my bill is wrong?

If you think there's an error on your bill:
- Ask your insurance company for your EOB for the relevant date of service
- Compare the EOB with your statement
- Contact our billing team with any discrepancies

You have the right to request an **itemized bill** that lists every service and charge in detail. We can mail or email one upon request.

## Is this a collections call?

No. This is a courtesy notification from Sarva Health's billing office to let you know a new statement has been generated. We reach out proactively so you're aware of your balance and can resolve it quickly. We are not a collections agency.
